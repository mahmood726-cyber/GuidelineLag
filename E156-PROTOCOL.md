## `[468]` Sparsentan in IgA Nephropathy: Living Meta-Analysis

**Type:** living-ma  |  ESTIMAND: OR for complete proteinuria remission + eGFR slope  
**Data:** 1 RCT (PROTECT), 404 patients; single-trial mode, CT.gov sourced

### 156-word body

Does sparsentan, a dual endothelin and angiotensin II receptor antagonist, reduce proteinuria and slow eGFR decline in IgA nephropathy? One randomized active-controlled trial is currently deployed in this living meta-analysis: PROTECT enrolled 404 patients with biopsy-proven IgA nephropathy and compared sparsentan against irbesartan. Single-trial estimate used published endpoints for proteinuria reduction and eGFR slope from the trial's pre-specified primary analyses. Complete remission of proteinuria was achieved more often with sparsentan than with irbesartan (odds ratio 2.88, 95% CI 1.93 to 4.33). The eGFR total slope favoured sparsentan with a mean difference of approximately 1.0 millilitre per minute per 1.73 square metres per year. Sparsentan reduces proteinuria and slows eGFR decline in IgA nephropathy more effectively than standard angiotensin receptor blockade. Long-term kidney-outcome trials are ongoing; hepatic monitoring is required, and placement relative to SGLT2 inhibitors in IgA nephropathy remains unclear.

We developed the Evidence Collider, an automated synthesis engine that cross-references 400,000+ clinical trials from AACT against 1,005 IHME global locations using the Socio-Demographic Index (SDI).
