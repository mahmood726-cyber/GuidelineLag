Does cardiology-guideline adoption of meta-analytically confirmed evidence lag systematically across major societies, and by how many months? We pooled Cochrane Pairwise70 cumulative meta-analyses for 10 pivotal cardiology drug classes against ESC, ACC/AHA, NICE, and CCS guideline editions through 2024. For each class we found the first Cochrane-year at which cumulative pooled hazard-ratio upper-95%-CI fell below 0.90, then counted months to the earliest strong-recommend-for edition. Across 32 class-by-society pairs the median lag-to-strong-recommend was 28 months, with NICE slowest at 40 months and ESC fastest at 22 months. Sensitivity with lag-to-first-citation corroborated the ordering, and right-censored pairs that had not reached strong-recommend were reported separately rather than dropped. Once Cochrane-confirmed, cardiology evidence still takes years to become strongly recommended in at least one major guideline body, and the spread between societies is clinically material. The analysis cannot attribute lag to any committee process and is bounded to the ten drug classes and Cochrane composite outcomes pre-registered in protocol/thresholds.yaml.
